Song Popularity Prediction

A Deep Learning project with Convolutional Neural Netorks.




The main problem is to estimate how popular a song is going to be according to its features. These features to be included are distributed in three main groups: general information about the song, music features analysis, and lyrics analysis. We designed three convolutional neural network architectures to try to detect what's important for a song's popularity. Based on our data, we detect that lyrics are quite relevant for the sake of this metric, and we extract a 3D figure of the lyrics, with PCA dimensionality reduction that helps to see that relevance. Our model gets to have a MSE of 0.0031 in lyrics testing, demonstrating that it is possible to try to predict a song's popularity. Resources:

News Summarization

A Twitter stream bot equipped with fine-tuned BERT summarizers to answer users' requests



Our project aims to develop a generative model able to autonomously generate concise and informative summaries for news in social media. After months of research and development, we present successful results that fulfil our initial objectives. Our generative model is capable of creating readable summaries that capture the essential points of news articles. To showcase the practical application of our model, we implement a real-world use case by deploying a Twitter bot that generates news summaries when provided with a URL. The bot processes the input text by employing the Flesch Readability Test and a "Legal" Bag of Words to filter out noise and generate legible summaries, answering the users' queries by citing the bot Twitter's account.

A US Senate Data Analysis

A Data Analysis of the US Senate social media relations, with a publicly available big dataset



Social media, specifically Twitter, plays a crucial role in political discourse and communication. It is the window of voters to their candidates, and what they publish may determine their success in the elections. A deep analysis is needed in order to better understand the current situation and think of strategies to reach the audience. This project joins the creation of a self-made dataset, machine learning topic models, geography and a brand-new created popularity metric to explain the current political landscape and provide insights about the most influential senators and their discourse.

Tweet Classification

A semi-supervised Tweet Classifier using density clustering and Bi-LSTM Neural Networks.



Final Bachelor project as a researcher on the Speech Technology Group at UPM. It was also my first Data Science project. I built a semi supervised tweet classifier using HDBSCAN, UMAP and TF-IDF to cluster the tweet dataset. I later used the clusters to get an approximation of the topics, based on key words. Statify

An analysis of the cultural differences on Music Tastes.




Have you ever asked yourself what needs an artist to be on the Global Top? Is it the same for every country? How does countries culture influence artists success? In this project, I try to explain all these questions using a Python script (publicly available) and data provided by Spotify API.
I use the song features provided by Spotify: danceability, acoustics, key, speechiness, liveness, energy, valence, and instrumentals. In addition, I created a new interesting metric called versatility, a combination of the prior with unsupervised learning. Basically, all the features in an array form a point in an eight-dimensional space (a matrix of 8 items). If we use all the tracks of an artist, playlist, or whatever, we obtain a map of points that can be clustered and represented in 2 or 3 dimensions (in this case I have used HDBSCAN and UMAP). If the result is a small number of clusters, this indicates that the singer does not have a variety of registers, he would be monotone. Whereas in the other case, he/she would be considered to be versatile, varying their tracks frequently.
